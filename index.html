Growth, Inflation & Labor
Real GDP Growth (2018–2024)
Pre‑COVID → COVID → Recovery

GDP contracted sharply in 2020 (‑3.4%), rebounded in 2021 (5.9%), and then settled near 2–3% growth in 2022–2024.[web:71][web:73]

Inflation vs Unemployment (2018–2024)
Dual‑axis view

Post‑pandemic, inflation spiked as unemployment fell; since 2023, inflation is easing while unemployment edges up.[web:69][web:71]

Policy, Trade & Dollar
Federal Funds Rate Path
Monetary Policy

Policy rates moved from near‑zero (2020–2021) to above 5% by 2023, then began easing as inflation cooled.[web:71][web:78]

Deficit & Interest Burden
Fiscal Health

Pandemic‑era deficits exceeded 10% of GDP and interest costs have risen rapidly with higher rates and large debt.[web:79][web:71]

Trade Balance (Goods & Services)
External Sector

The USA runs a persistent trade deficit; 2024 gap is close to ‑$900B, mainly due to goods imports.[web:74][web:76]
